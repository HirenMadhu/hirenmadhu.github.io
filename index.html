Hiren Madhu

I am an incoming PhD student at Yale CS, where I will be advised by Professor Smita Krishnaswamy and Professor Rex Ying.

Earlier, I was an ECE pre-doctoral research fellow in Electrical Communication Engineering Department at the Indian Institute of Science, Bengaluru. I worked with Sundeep Prabhakar Chepuri on unsupervised representation learning for simplicial complexes.

Before my pre-doc stint, I was a Master of Technology (MTech) student specializing in Artificial Intelligence, advised by Sundeep Prabhakar Chepuri. My master's thesis focused on developing algorithms for simplicial representation learning without labeled data.

I began my research journey at LDRP Institute of Technology and Research (BE in Computer Engineering) under the guidance of Sandip Modha and Thomas Mandl, where I focused on the detection of hate speech in public conversational threads on social media.

My primary research interest is Machine Learning with Limited Labeled Data (ML3D), especially graph representation learning without labels.
