Days 1 – 3

Bangkok

Day 1 is the shopping marathon — start at Chatuchak right at opening to beat the heat. Day 2 leans into culture and sky bars. Day 3 closes with the Grand Palace, a traditional massage, sunset, and nightlife.

1Sun

Shopping Marathon

Sunday 12 July

A heavy day — six markets and a skincare store. Wear sneakers, carry water, take breaks. The geography flows north → central → east → north as the day progresses.

10:15 AM

Chatuchak Weekend Market

Over 10,000 stalls of clothes, accessories, antiques, souvenirs and food. Sunday-only — this is the unmissable item on Day 1. Three hours minimum; you'll wish you had more.

Getting thereGrab from Salil (~40 min, ~250 baht), or shuttle boat to ICONSIAM → BTS Gold Line → Silom Line → MRT to Chatuchak Park.
1:45 PM

Bang Sue Junction — the Red Building

Six floors of curated vintage right across the street from Chatuchak. Floors 3 & 4 are fashion (Levi's, Carhartt, military surplus, Y2K). The 6th-floor food court is where locals actually eat — cheap, authentic, air-conditioned.

Getting thereA 5-minute walk from Chatuchak's main entrance, directly across Kamphaeng Phet 2 Rd.
3:30 PM

Pratunam cluster

Platinum Fashion Mall (wholesale clothing across multiple buildings), Pratunam Market (street fashion, accessories, knock-offs), and Indra Square — all within walking distance of each other at the Ratchaprarop intersection.

Getting thereMRT from Chatuchak Park → Phaya Thai → transfer to BTS → one stop to Ratchathewi (~25 min total). Or a single Grab.
5:30 PM

Tofu Skincare

Multi-floor store of affordable skincare, makeup, haircare and beauty. Reviewers regularly spend hours here — block out time. Deposit bags at the entrance to free up your hands.

Getting thereA 5-minute walk from Platinum Mall along Phetchaburi Rd.
6:30 PM

Again & Again Vintage

Influencer's secondhand store on Rama IV — vintage clothing, accessories, jewelry. Closes 7 PM, so this is the tight one in the day. Allow 30 minutes; bargain on the prices.

Getting thereGrab from Tofu Skincare (~15–20 min in traffic). It's at the Suanplern Market area on Rama IV.
Heads-up: some reviews flag overpricing and mixed authenticity — inspect items carefully before paying.
8:00 PM

Jodd Fair Ratchada

Bangkok's most-loved night market — 70+ food stalls (try the giant grilled river prawns and croc skewers), youthful vibe, neon-lit. Dinner happens here. Open until 1 AM.

Getting thereFrom Again & Again area: MRT from Khlong Toei or Lumphini → Phra Ram 9 station (~15 min). Or Grab.
11:00 PM

Khao San Road · optional

The famous backpacker walking street. Bars, street food, clubs, fire shows, tattoo parlors. Skip if Day 1 has been too much — there's another night to do this.

Getting thereGrab from Jodd Fair (~25 min in light evening traffic).
2Mon

Temples, Chinatown, LV & the Light Museum

Monday 13 July

Slower morning, building to one of the trip's visual highlights — the Akara Sky Hanuman light installation on the 61st floor at sunset.

9:00 AM

Wat Arun — Temple of Dawn

The porcelain-studded central prang in morning light, before the heat builds. Climb partway up for river views.

Getting thereSalil shuttle boat or Grab to Sathorn pier → Chao Phraya Express Boat north to Tha Tien → small cross-river ferry to Wat Arun.
11:30 AM

Sri Maha Mariamman

A vivid South Indian Hindu temple in the Silom district — free to enter, shoes off, dress respectfully.

Getting thereFerry back across, then short Grab or BTS to Sala Daeng / Sam Yan.
2:30 PM

Chinatown & Talad Noi by day

Yaowarat in daylight is a different city: Sino-Portuguese shophouses, Wat Mangkon, the Talad Noi street-art alleys, and old workshops. Calmer than the night and easier to photograph.

Getting thereMRT to Wat Mangkon station, in the heart of Chinatown.
5:00 PM

Capybara Coffee Silom

Pet the capybaras (and the meerkats, if they bring one out). 400 baht entry + a drink, ~30-minute slot. Book ahead since it fills up.

Getting thereOn Soi Silom 26, near BTS Surasak — easy by Grab or BTS from Chinatown.
6:15 PM

LV The Place — Visionary Journeys

Louis Vuitton's immersive exhibition at Gaysorn Amarin. Vintage trunks, archival pieces, futuristic interactive rooms, a tunnel entrance, and a small gift on the way out. Entry is free; the café needs a reservation. Closes 8 PM.

Getting thereBTS Sala Daeng → Phloen Chit (3 stops); Gaysorn Amarin is at the station exit.
Pre-book: the café reservation fills up days out. Book online before this day.
8:00 PM

Akara Sky Hanuman · light museum visit

Floors 58–61 of One City Centre, with themed glass rooms, illusion installations, and a ballroom view over Bangkok at sunset. Just the museum visit — no dinner package. Around 1.5–2 hours.

Getting thereA 5-minute walk from Gaysorn Amarin — One City Centre is right next door.
Pre-book: the museum-only ticket needs reserving — check available time slots online ahead of time.
10:00 PM

Dinner around Ploenchit

Plenty of restaurants in the Ploenchit / Central Embassy area for a late dinner after the light museum. Then back to Salil to rest.

Getting thereBTS Phloen Chit → Saphan Taksin → Grab back to Salil. Or Grab directly.
3Tue

Grand Palace, Massage, Sirocco, Patpong

Tuesday 14 July

Last Bangkok day. Big culture in the morning, recovery in the afternoon, atmosphere in the evening. Don't push too late — Phuket flight tomorrow at 10:20 AM.

8:30 AM

Grand Palace & Wat Phra Kaew

Arrive at opening to beat heat and crowds. The Emerald Buddha is Thailand's most sacred image. No photos in the chapel. Strict dress code — shoulders and knees covered.

Getting thereSalil to Sathorn pier, then Chao Phraya Express Boat north to Tha Chang — the most scenic way to arrive.
11:30 AM

Wat Pho — Reclining Buddha

A walk from the Grand Palace. The 46-metre Reclining Buddha is iconic — gold-leafed, lying on its side, with mother-of-pearl-inlaid feet.

Getting there5-minute walk from the Grand Palace exit.
2:00 PM

Wat Pho Massage School

Traditional Thai massage at the spot where the technique is officially taught. The most famous traditional massage place in Bangkok — usually a short wait, no booking needed. ~500–700 baht for an hour.

Getting thereInside the Wat Pho temple compound — no transit needed.
6:30 PM

Sirocco at Lebua State Tower

The famous golden-dome rooftop bar — featured in The Hangover Part II. Sunset cocktails, panoramic skyline. Right near Salil.

Getting there10-minute walk or short Grab from Salil. BTS Saphan Taksin is at the base of the building.
Dress code: smart casual. No shorts, no flip-flops, no sleeveless tops for men. Pack a collared shirt.
9:30 PM

Patpong walking street

Bangkok's most notorious nightlife strip — a night market layered over an adult-entertainment area. Ping pong shows are the area's specialty.

Getting there15-minute walk or 5-minute Grab from Sirocco. Right in Silom.
Be careful: confirm the entry fee and drink-minimum prices before sitting down at any show — inflated bills and pushy touts are common scams. Have an exit plan.

Days 4 – 7

Phuket

Old Town as home base, a big animal-and-spa day in the middle, and the iconic Phi Phi boat trip near the end.

4Wed

Arrive, Big Buddha, Old Town, Chillva

Wednesday 15 July

Land midday, hit the island's iconic hilltop, then settle into Old Town and find dinner at a great night market.

1:30 PM

The Big Buddha

A 45-metre marble statue on a hilltop with 360° views. Front access has been restricted recently, but it's still viewable. Watch for monkeys. Roughly on the way from the airport to Old Town.

Getting therePre-arranged car or Grab from HKT airport (~45 min). Stop here before continuing to Old Town.
4:30 PM

Old Town stroll + Hey! Mango

Wander the Sino-Portuguese shophouse streets — Thalang, Dibuk, Soi Rommanee. Hey! Mango on Soi Rommanee is the famous mango-smoothie spot. Simple, fresh, photogenic.

Getting thereFrom your hotel — everything in Old Town is walkable within 10–15 minutes.
6:00 PM

Chillva Market — dinner

Phuket's coolest night market — upcycled shipping-container shops, 50+ food stalls (the mango squid spicy salad is a known winner), live music. More relaxed than Bangkok night markets. Open Wed–Sat.

Getting there~10-minute Grab from Old Town. Located in the Ratsada area of Phuket Town.
5Thu

Lotus Cafe, Tigers, Spa, Patong

Thursday 16 July

The busiest Phuket day — four very different experiences. Tight but doable. A hired car/driver for the day makes the logistics easier than chaining Grabs.

8:30 AM

Ma Doo Bua Cafe — breakfast

The Instagram-famous lotus-pond cafe. Take your own photos for free (the paid photo packages are pricey). Decent coffee and Thai breakfast; the setting is the point.

Getting there~30-minute drive north of Old Town, in Thalang. Best by hired car (then continue to Tiger Kingdom).
Mixed reviews (3.7) — the experience is heavily commercialized. Go early before the photo lines build.
10:30 AM

Tiger Kingdom Phuket

Choose the smallest tiger encounter for the closest, calmest interaction. Trainers walk you through it; closed-toe shoes recommended; hand-washing before and after. ~10 minutes inside the enclosure once your turn comes up.

Getting there~25-minute drive from Ma Doo Bua, in Kathu district between Phuket Town and Patong.
Honest note: animal-welfare orgs criticize big-cat petting operations, and reviews are mixed (3.9). Worth knowing.
2:00 PM

Spa — half day

3–4 hours of massage and treatments. Phuket spas are excellent and well-priced — book at your hotel's spa or a well-reviewed nearby one. A genuine reset before the Phi Phi day.

Getting thereMost spas arrange pickup, or a short Grab. Walkable if you book near Old Town.
8:00 PM

Patong Walking Street (Bangla Road)

Phuket's neon-drenched nightlife strip. Bars, clubs, neon signs, fire-breathers, and chaos. Dinner along the way — plenty of options. The walking street really kicks in around 9 PM.

Getting there~30-minute Grab from Old Town to Patong. Plan on Grabbing back too, late-night.
Same caution as Bangkok's Patpong: always confirm drink prices and entry fees before sitting down.
6Fri

Phi Phi Islands & Pi Leh Lagoon

Friday 17 July

The big island-hopping day — book a tour that explicitly includes Pi Leh Bay, since not all of them do.

FULL DAY

Phi Phi + Pi Leh + Maya Bay

Limestone cliffs rising out of turquoise water, snorkeling stops, the iconic Maya Bay, Monkey Beach, and the dramatic enclosed lagoon at Pi Leh on Phi Phi Leh. Sunrise tours get you there before the crowds.

Getting thereTour minivan pickup at your hotel (~7:00–7:30 AM) → Rassada Pier → speedboat. Total day ~10 hours.
Pre-book: this is the single excursion that really needs reserving in advance. Confirm Pi Leh Bay is included.

What to eat

The edible itinerary

Six dishes to actively hunt down — all easy to find, all easy to make vegetarian if you ask the right way.

Pad Thai

ผัดไทย

Stir-fried rice noodles with tofu, egg, peanuts. Ask for no fish sauce or dried shrimp to keep it veg.

Green Curry

แกงเขียวหวาน

Coconut curry with tofu and vegetables. Check the paste — some contain shrimp.

Mango Sticky Rice

ข้าวเหนียวมะม่วง

Sweet coconut sticky rice with ripe mango. Naturally vegetarian — and a must.

Som Tam

ส้มตำ

Pounded green papaya salad — bright, hot and sour. Ask for it without fish sauce and dried shrimp.

Khao Soi

ข้าวซอย

Northern coconut-curry noodle soup, crispy noodles on top. Increasingly served in veg versions.

Street Food Grazing

อาหารริมทาง

Spring rolls, fried tofu, grilled corn, coconut ice cream — Jodd Fair and Chillva are where it lives.

🌿

Eating vegetarian — the key phrases

Say "gin jay" for strict vegetarian/vegan (no meat, egg, dairy or pungent veg), or "gin mangsawirat" for vegetarian that may include egg and dairy. The things that catch people out are fish sauce (nam pla), oyster sauce and dried shrimp used as background seasoning — "mai sai nam pla" means no fish sauce. Look for yellow flags marking jay food. Phuket Old Town in particular is full of vegetarian-friendly cafés.

Logistics

Getting around

Bangkok runs on trains, river ferries and Grab. Phuket runs on cars. A hired driver for Day 5 saves real time.

In Bangkok

BTS, MRT, ferries, Grab

The BTS Skytrain and MRT subway skip the notorious traffic — use them whenever you can. The Chao Phraya Express Boat from Sathorn pier reaches all the riverside temples. Salil's free shuttle boat connects you to ICONSIAM and the BTS Gold Line. Grab fills in the gaps with fixed fares.

In Phuket

You'll need wheels

No train, sights are spread out. Grab and metered taxis for short hops; strongly consider a hired car with driver for Day 5 — Ma Doo Bua → Tiger Kingdom → spa → Patong chains together much faster with one driver waiting between stops.

Between cities

Two domestic flights

Both legs are Thai Vietjet, ~1 hr 30 min. Outbound Wed Jul 15 at 10:20 AM (lands 11:55 AM). Return Sat Jul 18 at 12:35 PM (lands 2:05 PM). Book ahead for the best fares.

The final hop

Walk to the gate

From the Hyatt Regency Suvarnabhumi, it's a covered walkway under 10 minutes to the terminal. No taxi needed for the 2:35 AM departure — just walk over around 11:00 PM.

Before you go

Practical notes

i

July is rainy season on the Andaman coast

Expect warm days with passing downpours. Keep the Phi Phi day flexible — rough seas can cancel boats.

i

Temple dress code

Shoulders and knees covered at all temples. The Grand Palace enforces this strictly. Sirocco enforces smart casual after dark.

i

Cash + Grab

Street food, ferries, tuk-tuks and markets are cash-based. Install Grab before you land. 7-Elevens and ATMs are everywhere.

i

Sunday is the only Chatuchak day

Day 1 has to be the shopping marathon — that's why the itinerary opens with it.

i

Patpong & Bangla Road

Always confirm entry fees and drink prices before sitting at any show. Carry small bills. Have an exit plan.

i

Sun protection

Especially for Phi Phi and the Big Buddha. Reef-safe sunscreen for the boat day.

What to book ahead

  1. LV The Place café reservation — book 1–2 days ahead. Day 2 (Mon).
  2. Akara Sky Hanuman museum slot — book through their website. Day 2 evening.
  3. Capybara Coffee Silom slot — fills up. Day 2 late afternoon.
  4. Phi Phi tour with Pi Leh Bay included — book this week. Day 6 (Fri).
