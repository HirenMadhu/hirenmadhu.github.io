The shape of the trip

Two halves, one easy rhythm

Days 1 – 3

Bangkok

Start early each morning — it beats both the heat and the crowds, especially at the Grand Palace.

1Sat

Grand Palace & the Old City

Saturday 12 July

8:30 AM

Temple of the Emerald Buddha

Thailand's most sacred temple, inside the Grand Palace complex. Arrive right at opening. Strict dress code — shoulders and knees covered.

Getting thereFrom most central hotels, a metered taxi or Grab is 15–25 min. Or take the Chao Phraya Express Boat to Tha Chang pier, a short walk away.
12:00 PM

Wat Suthat & the Giant Swing

A quieter royal temple with a huge bronze Buddha and story-telling murals. The towering red Giant Swing stands right out front.

Getting thereAbout a 10-min tuk-tuk or taxi ride east of the Grand Palace — agree the fare first, or use Grab.
1:30 PM

Wat Ratchabophit

Underrated and often nearly empty — intricate tilework and a calm courtyard. There's a good coffee shop across the street.

Getting thereA 5-min walk from Wat Suthat — the whole Old City is compact and walkable between stops.
2Sun

Riverside & Chinatown

Sunday 13 July

9:00 AM

Wat Arun — Temple of Dawn

Climb partway up the porcelain-studded central prang for views over the river. Beautiful in the morning light before the heat builds.

Getting thereTake the cross-river ferry from Tha Tien pier — it runs every few minutes and costs only a few baht.
12:30 PM

Sri Maha Mariamman Temple

A vivid South Indian Hindu temple in the Silom district — free to enter, shoes off, dress respectfully.

Getting thereCross back on the ferry, then a short taxi/Grab ride to Silom — or the MRT to Sam Yan, then a 10-min walk.
6:30 PM

Yaowarat — Chinatown after dark

The heart of Bangkok's street food scene. Come hungry and graze your way down the neon-lit main road — noodle soups, grilled skewers, fresh fruit.

Getting thereThe MRT Blue Line stops right at Wat Mangkon station, in the middle of Chinatown. Easiest after-dark option.
3Mon

Chatuchak Market, then fly south

Monday 14 July

9:30 AM

Chatuchak Weekend Market

Over 10,000 stalls of clothes, crafts, antiques and food. Go early before the heat peaks. Note: 14 July is a Monday, when Chatuchak is closed — swap in the Pratunam shopping district, or visit Chatuchak's adjacent daily JJ Mall / weekend-only sections won't be open, so Pratunam is the cleaner choice.

Getting thereFor Pratunam: BTS to Chit Lom or Ratchathewi. For the market area: MRT to Chatuchak Park or BTS to Mo Chit.
1:30 PM

Last Bangkok lunch, then the airport

A relaxed Thai meal before heading out. Then the short flight to Phuket — about 1 hr 25 min.

Getting thereSuvarnabhumi (BKK) or Don Muang (DMK) depending on airline. Allow 1 hr by taxi from the city, more in traffic. The Airport Rail Link serves Suvarnabhumi.
✈

Bangkok → Phuket

A 1 hr 25 min domestic flight. AirAsia, Thai Lion, Nok Air and Thai Vietjet all fly it many times daily — cheapest booked a few weeks ahead. An evening flight on the 14th lands you in Phuket in time to settle in.

Days 4 – 7

Phuket

A gentler pace: Old Town to settle in, a spa day in the middle, one big island trip, and the Big Buddha on your way out.

4Mon

Old Town — arrive & settle in

Monday 14 July · evening

EVENING

Phuket Old Town

Colourful Sino-Portuguese shophouses, cafés and small boutiques — very walkable and photogenic, especially as the lights come on. A low-key first evening: stroll the streets, find dinner, no transport to sort out.

Getting thereFrom the airport, a metered taxi, airport bus, or Grab to Old Town takes around 45 min–1 hr. Basing yourself in or near Old Town keeps this evening effortless.
5Tue

Spa day — the deliberate slow point

Tuesday 15 July

ALL DAY

Massage & wellness

A day built around rest, set between Old Town and the big boat day. Phuket does spas exceptionally well and affordably — from hour-long massages you can book same-day, to half- or full-day packages at resorts. Pair it with your hotel pool to keep it fully restful.

Good to knowNo need to book far ahead — decide on the ground based on where you're staying. Many spas will arrange pickup, or it's a short Grab ride.
6Wed

Phi Phi Islands boat trip

Wednesday 16 July

FULL DAY

Phi Phi & Maya Bay

The iconic island-hopping day — limestone cliffs straight out of turquoise water, snorkeling stops, Maya Bay. Sunrise tours mean an early start but get you there before the crowds.

Getting thereTours include hotel pickup by minivan to the pier, then speedboat. Book this one in advance — it's the single excursion that really needs reserving.
7Thu

Big Buddha, then fly home

Thursday 17 July → onward

MORNING

The Big Buddha

A 45-metre marble statue on a hilltop with 360° views over the island and its bays. Front access has been restricted recently, but it's still viewable — and the panorama is the real draw. Watch for monkeys.

Getting thereA scenic winding drive up from the Chalong area — easiest by Grab or a hired car/driver. Roughly on the way back toward the airport side of the island.

Your dates run 12–19 July, so there's a flexible final stretch — an extra slow morning, a quiet beach swim, or more Old Town café-hopping before the flight back to Bangkok for the international connection.

What to eat

The edible itinerary

A short list of dishes to actively hunt down — all of them easy to find, and all easy to get vegetarian if you ask.

Pad Thai

ผัดไทย

Stir-fried rice noodles with tofu, egg and peanuts. Ask for no fish sauce or dried shrimp to keep it veg.

Green Curry

แกงเขียวหวาน

Coconut curry with tofu and vegetables. Check the paste — some contain shrimp.

Mango Sticky Rice

ข้าวเหนียวมะม่วง

Sweet coconut sticky rice with ripe mango. Naturally vegetarian — and a must.

Som Tam

ส้มตำ

Pounded green papaya salad — bright, hot and sour. Ask for it without fish sauce and dried shrimp.

Khao Soi

ข้าวซอย

Northern coconut-curry noodle soup, crispy noodles on top. Increasingly served in veg versions.

Street Food Grazing

อาหารริมทาง

Spring rolls, fried tofu, grilled corn, coconut ice cream — Chinatown and Phuket's markets are the place.

🌿

Eating vegetarian — the key phrases

Say "gin jay" for strict vegetarian/vegan (no meat, egg, dairy or pungent veg), or "gin mangsawirat" for vegetarian that may include egg and dairy. The things that catch people out are fish sauce (nam pla), oyster sauce and dried shrimp used as background seasoning — "mai sai nam pla" means no fish sauce. Look for yellow flags marking jay food. Phuket Old Town in particular is full of vegetarian-friendly cafés.

Logistics

Getting around

Two very different cities — Bangkok runs on trains and boats, Phuket on cars.

In Bangkok

Trains, boats & Grab

The BTS Skytrain and MRT subway are fast, cheap and skip the notorious traffic — use them whenever you can. The Chao Phraya Express Boat links many riverside temples. For everything else, the Grab app gives you fair, fixed fares without haggling. Tuk-tuks are fun but agree the price first.

In Phuket

You'll need wheels

Phuket has no train and sights are spread far apart, so plan on Grab, metered taxis, or a hired car with driver for the day. Day tours and many spas include hotel pickup. Scooters are common but only if you're confident — the roads can be rough.

Between cities

The domestic flight

Bangkok–Phuket is a 1 hr 25 min hop, flown many times daily by AirAsia, Thai Lion, Nok Air and Thai Vietjet. Book a few weeks ahead for the best fares. Check whether you depart Suvarnabhumi or Don Muang — they're on opposite sides of Bangkok.

Where to base yourself

Phuket: stay central or calm

Old Town, Kata or Karon all suit this itinerary and are far calmer than the Patong nightlife strip. Old Town makes day 4 effortless and puts you among the best vegetarian cafés. Wherever you land, you'll still rely on Grab to reach beaches and tours.

Before you go

Practical notes

i

July is rainy season

The Andaman coast sees monsoon weather roughly May–October. Expect warm days with passing downpours, and keep the Phi Phi day flexible — rough seas can cancel boats. Build in a buffer.

i

Temple dress code

Shoulders and knees covered at all temples, shoes off where indicated. The Grand Palace enforces this strictly — bring a light layer.

i

Book Phi Phi ahead

It's the one excursion worth reserving before you arrive. The spa day, by contrast, can be arranged on the ground.

i

Chatuchak is weekends only

14 July is a Monday, so the market is closed — the site swaps in the Pratunam shopping district for that morning.

i

Carry small cash

Street food, ferries, tuk-tuks and markets are cash-based. Cards work in malls, hotels and bigger restaurants; 7-Elevens and ATMs are everywhere.

i

Get the Grab app

Set it up before you land. It removes fare haggling in both cities and is the simplest way to get around Phuket.
