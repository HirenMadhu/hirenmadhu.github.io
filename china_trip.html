The Route

2,400 km through five provinces — click any marker for details

From Shenzhen northwest through Guangxi's dreamlike karst country, into Guizhou's minority highlands, north to Chongqing's fiery street-food scene, and east to Zhangjiajie's sandstone pillars that inspired Avatar.

Guangdong→ Guangxi→ Guizhou→ Chongqing→ Hunan
Day by Day — Road Trip
A note on the schedule With 9 days (2 in Shenzhen, 7 on the road) covering ~2,400 km, some days involve serious driving. Two days have 6+ hours; the rest are relaxed 3–5 hour stints with plenty of exploration time.

Shenzhen — Arrival & Exploration

01
FRI · JUL 11

Arrive in Shenzhen

Arrival day — no driving

Land, settle in, ease into China. Head to Dongmen Old Street for a buzzing night market — grilled skewers, stinky tofu, fresh sugar cane juice. Walk Shenzhen Bay Park at sunset for views across to Hong Kong.

Dongmen night marketShenzhen Bay sunset
02
SAT · JUL 12

Explore Shenzhen & Prep

City day — pick up car late afternoon

Morning at Dafen Oil Painting Village — thousands of artists mass-producing replicas of masterpieces. Afternoon in Nantou Ancient City (南头古城), 1,700-year-old walls now filled with galleries and street art. Pick up your rental car. Download Gaode Maps offline. Stock up on water, snacks, a power bank.

Guangxi — Karst Peaks & Rice Terraces

03
SUN · JUL 13

Shenzhen → Yangshuo

~630 km~7 hours via G78

Depart early. Watch for the transformation crossing into Guangxi — karst peaks rise like shark fins from flat paddies. Arrive by mid-afternoon. Find a side-alley restaurant for beer fish (啤酒鱼) — river fish braised in local beer with tomatoes and peppers.

First karst peaksBeer fish 啤酒鱼Li River sunset
04
MON · JUL 14

Yangshuo Countryside → Longji Rice Terraces

Morning: Yulong River by bikeAfternoon: 150 km north

Early bike ride along the Yulong River — water buffalo, bamboo rafts, karst peaks framing every turn. After lunch, drive to Longsheng. The Dragon's Backbone Terraces in July are electric green, carved over 700 years by the Zhuang and Red Yao peoples. Stay overnight in a wooden guesthouse on the terraces.

Yulong River cyclingRed Yao long-hair villagesLongji Terraces 龙脊梯田

Guizhou — Minority Villages & Hidden Mountains

05
TUE · JUL 15

Longji → Sanjiang Dong Villages → Kaili

~350 km~5.5 hours with stops

Cross into Guizhou through Dong minority country. Stop at the Chengyang Wind-Rain Bridge — a covered wooden bridge built without nails, with pavilions where old men play cards and smoke pipes. The surrounding villages have stilt houses and soaring drum towers. Try Dong sour fish (酸鱼) for lunch. Arrive in Kaili — gateway to Miao country — by evening.

Chengyang Wind-Rain BridgeDong drum towersDong sour fish 酸鱼
06
WED · JUL 16

Xijiang Thousand Household Miao Village

35 km from KailiFull day — overnight in village

The cultural centrepiece. Over 1,200 wooden stilt houses cascading down a river valley. Watch silversmiths forge jewelry, catch lusheng reed-pipe performances, wander back lanes where grandmothers embroider in doorways. Stay overnight — when every rooftop lights up at dusk, the valley glows amber. Dinner: acid soup fish (酸汤鱼), fiery fermented tomato broth with river fish.

1,200 Miao stilt housesSilver jewelryAcid soup fish 酸汤鱼

Chongqing — The Mountain City

07
THU · JUL 17

Xijiang → Chongqing

~620 km~8 hours — the marathon leg

The longest day. Northwest through Guizhou's dramatic interior — misty gorges, terraced hillsides of corn and tobacco, small market towns that see no outside visitors. As you cross into Chongqing municipality, mountains give way to the humid Sichuan Basin. Arrive by evening. Find a streetside hotpot joint (火锅) — bubbling málà broth of Sichuan peppercorn and dried chili oil.

Guizhou mountain roadsRural market townsChongqing hotpot 火锅
08
FRI · JUL 18

Chongqing Exploration

Rest day — no highway driving

China's most vertigo-inducing megacity — roads stacking on roads, where the 1st floor of one building is the 15th of the next. Morning at Ciqikou Ancient Town (磁器口). Ride the cross-river cable car over the Yangtze. Try xiaomian noodles (小面) for breakfast, grilled skewers for lunch. Evening: south bank skyline view when the peninsula blazes between two rivers.

Hunan — The Avatar Mountains

09
SAT · JUL 19

Chongqing → Zhangjiajie

~400 km~6 hours via G65

Final leg east into Hunan. The expressway cuts through dramatic gorge country — towering limestone, long tunnels, bridges over deep valleys. Arrive by early afternoon. Take the Bailong Elevator up the cliff face to Yuanjiajie plateau. Walk to the "Hallelujah Mountain" viewpoint — over 3,000 sandstone pillars rising through clouds. The trip ends where the earth touches the sky.

Bailong ElevatorHallelujah Mountain3,000+ sandstone pillars

Alternative: By High-Speed Train

Same destinations, no driving. China's HSR network connects all these stops — and the Chongqing → Zhangjiajie line opened in July 2025. You trade flexibility for zero driving fatigue and a completely different kind of scenery through the window.

Train Timeline — Same 9 Days
Day 1 — Fri Jul 11

Arrive in Shenzhen

Same as road trip. Explore Dongmen night market, Shenzhen Bay sunset. No train needed today.

Day 2 — Sat Jul 12

Shenzhen → Yangshuo

Morning: explore Dafen Oil Painting Village and Nantou Ancient City. Afternoon: take the HSR direct to Yangshuo station.

🚄 Shenzhen North → Yangshuo · ~3h · ¥232

Arrive by evening, explore Yangshuo's back alleys, beer fish for dinner. Saves a full driving day.

Day 3 — Sun Jul 13

Yangshuo → Longji Rice Terraces → Guilin

Morning: bike ride along the Yulong River. Afternoon: hire a local driver or take a shared minibus to Longji Terraces (~2.5 hrs). Hike the terraces, stay overnight in a guesthouse in Ping'an or Dazhai village. Note: no train goes to Longji — this leg requires a car/minibus from Guilin or Yangshuo.

Day 4 — Mon Jul 14

Longji → Guilin → Kaili

Morning on the terraces. Return to Guilin by minibus, then take the HSR via the Guiyang–Guangzhou line, which stops at Sanjiang South (for Dong villages) and continues to Kaili South.

🚐 Longji → Guilin · ~1.5h minibus 🚄 Guilin → Kaili South · ~3h via Sanjiang · ¥175

Alternatively, break at Sanjiang South station and hire a local taxi to visit Chengyang Wind-Rain Bridge (20 min from station), then catch a later train to Kaili.

Day 5 — Tue Jul 15

Xijiang Miao Village — Full Day

Same as road trip. Local bus from Kaili to Xijiang (~1 hr, ¥17). Full day immersion in the world's largest Miao settlement. Stay overnight in the village. Silver workshops, lusheng music, acid soup fish, amber rooftop lights at dusk.

Day 6 — Wed Jul 16

Xijiang → Kaili → Chongqing

Morning in Xijiang, then bus back to Kaili. Take the HSR to Guiyang (35 min), then change to the Chongqing-bound bullet train.

🚄 Kaili South → Guiyang North · 35 min · ¥50 🚄 Guiyang North → Chongqing West · 2h · ¥139

Arrive Chongqing by afternoon — far less tiring than the 8-hour drive. Evening: Chongqing hotpot.

Day 7 — Thu Jul 17

Chongqing Exploration

Full rest day same as road trip. Ciqikou Ancient Town, Yangtze cable car, xiaomian noodles, south-bank skyline at night. No train today.

Day 8 — Fri Jul 18

Chongqing → Zhangjiajie

Take the brand-new Chongqing–Zhangjiajie HSR (opened July 2025). Arrive in just 2 hours and spend the whole afternoon in the park.

🚄 Chongqing East → Zhangjiajie West · 2h · ¥215

Bailong Elevator, Yuanjiajie plateau, Hallelujah Mountain viewpoint. Stay near Wulingyuan gate.

Day 9 — Sat Jul 19

Zhangjiajie — Full Day Hiking

Golden Whip Stream (7.5 km valley walk), Tianzi Mountain panoramic views, sea of clouds. The big advantage of the train route: you get an extra full day in Zhangjiajie instead of spending it driving. Depart from Zhangjiajie Hehua Airport or take HSR to Changsha (1.5h) for more flight options.

The train advantage The HSR route gives you a full extra day to explore (no 8-hour Kaili→Chongqing drive, no 6-hour Chongqing→Zhangjiajie drive). The trade-off: you lose the freedom to stop at random villages, pull over at viewpoints, and drive the spectacular mountain roads. The rural roads through Guizhou are half the adventure by car — you won't get that from a train window. Both options are excellent; it depends on whether you want the journey to be part of the experience.

Before You Go

Navigation

Download Gaode Maps (高德地图) offline. Google Maps doesn't work reliably. Baidu Maps is backup. Program each day's route the night before.

Car Rental / Train Tickets

Car: book via Zuche or eHi, confirm one-way drop-off. Trains: book via Trip.com or 12306 app (needs Chinese phone number). Book 2–3 weeks ahead for summer.

Payment

Cash is nearly extinct. Set up WeChat Pay or Alipay — both support international cards. Small cash stash for remote Guizhou villages.

Summer Weather

Afternoon thunderstorms in Guizhou, Chongqing, Hunan. Mountain roads get slippery. Start early, pack rain jacket. July: 28–38°C. Chongqing is a furnace.

Language

Outside Shenzhen, English is rare. Offline translator essential. Key: 你好 (hello), 谢谢 (thanks), 多少钱 (how much?). Point at menu photos.

Connectivity

Local SIM at Shenzhen airport (China Mobile/Unicom). Install VPN before entering China if you need Google, Instagram, WhatsApp etc.
